Introduction


Animal Cruelty is defined as any intentional/repeated behavior that causes physical and mental distress in animals, resulting to pain, suffering or death. Examples of animal cruelty includes animal neglect, violence, mistreatment, any form of abuse and placed in imminent danger. [1] In the Philippines, there are certain laws regarding the protection of animal welfare. RA No. 8485 states that any person who abuses/mistreat animals will be punished through imprisonment or a fine. Even with the law being passed, it is not implemented properly. Many animals still suffer from torture and neglect from its owners. Continuation of these activities can cause distress, trauma and life-long pain among animals. This project aims to protect and prevent animal abuse. Which will end the suffering and all illegal killings on animals. It also aims to inform others about the cruelty that is still present in our society today and to raise awareness about stopping animal cruelty.
